Common Questions

Sprinkler Repair FAQ

The cost of sprinkler repair depends on the specific problem. Minor repairs and sprinkler tune ups can start around $100, while larger repairs involving underground leaks, valve replacements, manifold repairs, or extensive digging can cost $600 or more depending on labor and replacement parts. For most homeowners, the average sprinkler repair service call falls between $100 and $300.

Low sprinkler pressure can be caused by broken sprinkler heads, clogged nozzles, leaking pipes, failing valves, main line leaks, roots damaging irrigation lines, well pump problems, backflow issues, or low water pressure. If your sprinklers are not spraying as far as they used to or dry spots are developing in your lawn, a professional sprinkler inspection can identify the cause and restore proper performance.

A sprinkler zone may fail to operate because of a faulty solenoid, bad valve, wiring issue, controller problem, or lack of water flow. Troubleshooting the valve and electrical components is usually the first step in identifying the problem.

A constantly wet area is often caused by a leaking sprinkler valve, broken underground pipe, damaged fitting, or a main line leak. These problems can waste water and increase utility bills if not repaired quickly.
