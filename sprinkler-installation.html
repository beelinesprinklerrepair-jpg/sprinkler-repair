Sprinkler Installation Near Me — Treasure Valley, Idaho

Idaho gets around 12 inches of rain a year. Without irrigation, a lawn in the Treasure Valley doesn't make it through summer. A properly designed and installed sprinkler system is one of the best investments you can make in your property here, and one of the most common things we get called about is installing a brand new system on a home that never had one.

We've been installing sprinkler systems across Nampa, Boise, Meridian, and the rest of the valley since 2015. Whether you're on a new construction lot or an existing yard, the approach is the same: design the system for your specific property, install it with quality components, pressure test it, and walk you through everything before we leave.

New Construction Sprinkler Installation

The right time to install irrigation on a new build is before sod goes down. The grade is already set, trenching is clean, and getting the system in early saves time and money compared to retrofitting later. We work directly with general contractors and builders to coordinate installation as part of the construction timeline. We've done it on hundreds of new builds across the valley.

If you're a Nampa or Caldwell homeowner in a new subdivision, or building in Meridian or Star, give us a call early in the process. We can usually schedule around the construction timeline and have the system ready before landscaping starts.

Existing Yard Irrigation Installation

Adding sprinklers to an existing yard takes more planning than a new construction install. We assess the lot, map zone coverage for turf and bed areas, design around your actual water pressure, and install with minimal disruption to existing landscaping. Most existing home installs in the Treasure Valley are completed in one to two days. We restore affected areas after trenching so the yard looks clean when we're done.

What's Included in Every Installation

  • On-site property assessment and zone layout design
  • Rotor heads for turf zones, spray heads for smaller areas and beds
  • Drip irrigation for landscape beds, shrubs, and trees where applicable
  • Electric zone valves with individual shutoffs per zone
  • Irrigation controller installation and full programming
  • Backflow prevention device installation
  • Pressure testing before backfill
  • Full system test and zone-by-zone walk-through at completion

New Construction Install

Treasure Valley soil is heavy clay in most areas. It absorbs water slowly and will pool or run off if zones are programmed to run too long. We design zone run times and precipitation rates around Idaho soil conditions rather than generic national defaults. Your system will be set up to actually water efficiently from day one, not puddle in the street or leave dry spots in the corners.

Many irrigation problems start long before the first sprinkler head goes in. Poor zone design, incorrect pipe sizing, inadequate pressure calculations, and improper head spacing all lead to dry spots, overspray, runoff, and expensive repairs down the road. We think through the design upfront so you're not calling someone six months later to fix coverage issues.

Common Questions

Sprinkler Installation FAQ

How much does a new sprinkler system cost in the Treasure Valley? +
Most residential sprinkler installations run between $1,400 and $1,850 per zone. A typical neighborhood home in Nampa, Meridian, Boise, or Caldwell can be designed within 3 to 4 zones, which puts most installs in the $4,200 to $7,400 range. The final number depends on property size, water pressure, how many zones the yard requires, and whether drip irrigation is included for landscape beds. The best way to get an accurate price is a free on-site estimate.
How many irrigation zones do I need? +
It depends on your lot size, water pressure at the meter, and your landscaping layout. Most neighborhood homes in the Treasure Valley built by CBH, Hubble, Hayden, Lennar, KB, or similar builders can be covered within 3 to 4 zones. Larger properties with extensive turf, beds, and drip areas may need more. We figure this out during the free on-site estimate by measuring the yard and checking your water pressure.
How long does sprinkler installation take? +
Most residential sprinkler installations are completed in one to three days depending on the size of the property and the complexity of the project. New construction installs before sod typically go faster. Existing yards with established landscaping take a bit more time but we work carefully to minimize disruption.
Can you install sprinklers in an existing yard? +
Yes. We regularly install sprinkler systems in existing yards throughout Nampa, Boise, Meridian, and the rest of the Treasure Valley. Trenching is required, but we take steps to minimize disruption and restore affected areas when the installation is complete. Most homeowners are happy with how the yard looks once we're done.
Do I need a backflow prevention device? +
Yes. Most sprinkler systems require a backflow prevention device to protect the public water supply. We determine the appropriate device during the estimate and include it in every installation.
Can you install drip irrigation for my landscape beds? +
Yes. Drip irrigation is a great option for landscape beds, shrubs, trees, and other planting areas where traditional sprinkler heads are not the right solution. We can include drip zones as part of a full system install or add them to an existing system.
When is the best time to install a sprinkler system? +
For new construction, before sod goes down is ideal. For existing yards, spring and early summer are the most popular times since that's when lawns start needing water. We do installs throughout the season as long as the ground is not frozen. Call to check our current availability.
