Trailer welding repair across the Treasure Valley

Trailers break.
We weld them back together.

Trailers take more abuse than almost anything with wheels. They ride overloaded, hit potholes and washboard gravel, get backed into loading docks, and sit out in the weather until rust finds the welds. Sooner or later a crossmember cracks, a tongue bends, a spring hanger tears loose, or a ramp hinge gives out — usually the day you actually need the trailer.

That is where trailer weld repair comes in. Idaho Welding Service runs mobile trailer repair across the whole Treasure Valley — Boise, Nampa, Meridian, and the smaller towns and farm ground in between. Utility trailers, flatbeds, dump trailers, stock and horse trailers, equipment haulers, and toy haulers all get the same treatment: cracks cleaned back to good metal, welded right, and reinforced where the load actually runs.

Why mobile beats towing a broken trailer

Towing a trailer with a cracked frame or a broken hitch is a good way to turn a repair into an accident. A frame that is already failing does not get better with a load and a highway on-ramp behind it. Mobile trailer repair means the trailer stays parked while we fix it — no white-knuckle drive to a shop. Call or text photos of the damage with your location and you will get a straight answer on the fix and the cost before a truck rolls.

The common trailer repairs

The stuff that cracks, bends, and tears loose.

After enough trailers you learn where they fail. It is almost always the same handful of spots — the places that carry the load, take the road shock, or get slammed open and shut. Here is what comes across the jack stands most:

  • Cracked frames, crossmembers, and welded joints along the main rails
  • Broken or bent tongues, worn couplers, and hitch repairs
  • Axle mounts, U-bolt plates, and spring hangers that have torn loose
  • Bent, cracked, or worn-through ramps and ramp hinges
  • Fenders, gates, tailgates, and stake pockets
  • Rotted or cracked decking crossmembers and safety chain loops

Every kind of trailer

Work trailers and weekend trailers.

Work & farm trailers

Flatbeds, dump trailers, stock trailers, gooseneck and bumper-pull equipment haulers — the trailers that earn their keep are also the ones that break hardest. We handle cracked flatbed frames and headboards, dump trailer hoists and hinge points, worn ramp gates on equipment haulers, torn axle mounts, and the tired welds on old stock trailers that have hauled a lot of miles. If it is a working trailer holding up a job, mobile trailer repair gets it back under load instead of parked in a repair line.

Horse, toy & boat trailers

Recreational trailers are usually lighter built and often aluminum, which changes how they are fixed. Horse trailer floors, dividers, and frame cracks, toy hauler ramps and tie-downs, and boat trailer frames that live half their life wet all come through here. Aluminum trailer welding takes TIG or MIG and proper prep — aluminum fatigues and cracks differently than steel, so it gets welded for the metal it actually is, not treated like a steel trailer that happens to be shiny.

Common questions

Trailer repair FAQ.

Can you fix my trailer where it sits?

Yes. Most trailer weld repair happens right in your driveway, yard, shop, or on the job site. Towing a trailer with a cracked frame or a broken tongue is how a small problem turns into a wreck, so mobile trailer repair keeps it parked while we fix it. If the trailer can be moved safely, shop service is also an option.

What kinds of trailer repairs do you handle?

Cracked frames and crossmembers, broken tongues and couplers, worn hitches, bent or torn ramps and ramp hinges, axle mounts and spring hangers, fenders, gates, decking, and safety chain loops. Utility trailers, flatbeds, dump trailers, stock and horse trailers, equipment haulers, and toy haulers all come across the jack stands.

How much does trailer weld repair cost?

It depends on the trailer, the damage, and how much weld time is involved — a cracked chain loop is not the same job as a rebuilt tongue. The fastest way to get a straight answer is to text photos of the damage and your location to 208-409-0756 before any work starts.

Can you weld aluminum trailers?

Yes. We run TIG and MIG on aluminum for horse trailers, boat trailers, and lightweight utility trailers, along with steel work on everything else. Aluminum cracks and fatigues differently than steel, so it gets prepped and welded for the metal it actually is.

Is it safe to weld a cracked trailer frame?

A cracked frame, axle mount, or spring hanger is a real repair, not a cosmetic one, because it carries the load and the road shock. We clean the crack back to good metal, weld it properly, and reinforce it where it makes sense so it holds instead of cracking open again next season.

Do you take emergency or after-hours trailer repair calls?

Yes. A trailer usually breaks loaded, on a trip, or right before you need it. Call 208-409-0756 — if your trailer is down and holding up work or a haul, after-hours mobile calls are welcome.
