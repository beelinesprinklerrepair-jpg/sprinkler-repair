Common Sprinkler Problems We See in Kuna

Certain problems come up again and again in Kuna. The combination of fast-growing subdivisions, builder-budget installs, open lots, and cold winters creates a predictable set of irrigation issues:

  • Coverage gaps — Dry corners, bare edges, and strips along fences and under eaves are the most common complaint in Kuna. Builder head placement often misses the edges of lots entirely. Troubleshooting bad coverage
  • Main line leaks from shallow installs — Frost heave is harder on shallow installs. We regularly find cracked fittings and split PVC sections in Kuna subdivisions every spring. Big wet spot in the yard
  • Low pressure on some zones — Builder cost-cutting often means too many heads on a single zone. When pressure is spread too thin, heads don’t pop up fully and coverage suffers. Troubleshooting low pressure
  • Backflow damage after skipped blowouts — Backflow devices left with water over winter crack. We see this every season in Kuna, particularly on systems where the blowout was skipped or done improperly.
  • Controller programming errors on new systems — Many Kuna homeowners are dealing with irrigation for the first time. Incorrect zone runtimes, wrong watering days, and skipped seasonal adjustments add up to dead grass or overwatered soil. Valve box full of water
  • Seasonal startup issues — Skipping the spring turn-on is common in Kuna. Without a proper pressurization and zone check in April, problems that developed over winter go undetected until a zone fails mid-season.

Most sprinkler repair jobs in Kuna run between $100 and $300. We give you a straight answer on cost before any work begins, so there are no surprises.
Dry spots in Kuna subdivisions are almost always caused by builder-installed systems with overloaded zones, poorly aimed heads, or insufficient head spacing for the lot size. Open lots in Kuna also face more wind exposure, which dries turf faster and makes coverage gaps more visible. A zone-by-zone walkthrough can identify and fix the problem. We find this issue on the majority of newer Kuna homes we visit for the first time.
Yes. Main line leaks are one of the most common repairs we perform in Kuna. Shallow main line installs on newer construction are particularly vulnerable to frost heave and freeze-thaw damage over winter. We locate the leak, excavate, repair or replace the damaged section, test the full system, and backfill before leaving. If you have a wet spot in your yard that is not going away, a main line leak is a likely cause.
Schedule your winterization blowout in Kuna in late September to early October, before the first hard freeze. Kuna winters are cold enough to crack backflow devices, split PVC fittings, and damage valves if water is left standing in the system. Backflow devices and shallow-installed main lines are especially vulnerable. Do not wait until after a freeze to schedule — slots fill up fast in the fall.
Yes. We perform spring turn-ons throughout Kuna every season, typically starting in mid-April once irrigation water is available. A proper spring turn-on includes pressurizing the system slowly, running every zone, checking for freeze damage, adjusting any heads that have shifted, and calibrating the controller for the season. Many Kuna homeowners skip this step and then wonder why zones are not working correctly in June. It is worth doing every year.
