Commercial welding services, valley-wide

Commercial work runs
on a schedule.

On a commercial job, the welding rarely happens in a vacuum. There's a sequence, a set of plans, an inspection to hit, and half a dozen other trades all working toward the same deadline. The welder's job is to land the steel scope in its window, get it right the first time, and get out of the way so framing, glazing, or finish work can keep moving. That's how we run a commercial welding job.

Idaho Welding Service works with general contractors, site supers, and property managers across the whole Treasure Valley. We bring the truck, the welders, and the generator to your site, weld the connections and details on the plan, and keep our piece of the schedule from becoming the thing that holds everything else up.

Plans in, straight quote out

Most commercial calls start with a scope and a set of drawings. Send the plans, the detail, or text photos with the job site location and you'll get a straight quote on the work — the material, the weld time, and how it fits your schedule — before the truck ever rolls.

The steel a commercial site needs welded.

Commercial welding covers a wide range of work, from the structural connections that hold a building together to the railings, gates, and hardware that keep it code-compliant and running. We show up stocked to cut, fit, and weld steel, stainless, and aluminum on-site — and we build to the detail your engineer or GC provides, not a guess.

  • Handrail and guardrail welded and installed to the specified detail

  • Tenant-improvement steel — support framing, mounts, and infill

Two kinds of commercial welding

New builds and the buildings already standing.

New construction & tenant improvement

On new builds and TI work, we come in as the welding piece of a bigger project. That means reading the plans, welding structural connections and support steel to the engineered detail, and coordinating with the GC so our work is ready when the inspection or the next trade needs it. Railings, stairs, mounts, and infill steel all get welded to spec and left ready to sign off. We don't stamp the drawings — we execute the detail your engineer already did.

Maintenance & repair

Existing commercial properties break, wear out, and fail inspection too. Property managers, restaurants, retail shops, and warehouses call us for the loose handrail, the bent dock hardware, the sagging gate, the cracked equipment mount, or the rack that took a forklift hit. Most of this is on-site, scheduled around your hours so we're not welding in front of your customers, and handled in one trip whenever the scope allows.

Common questions

Commercial welding FAQ.

Do you work with general contractors on commercial jobs?

Yes. A lot of our commercial welding work comes through general contractors and site supers who need a welder to handle a specific scope on a build or tenant improvement. Send the plans, the detail, or a description of what's needed, and we'll give you a straight quote and slot into the schedule so we're not the trade holding everything else up.

Can you weld to our engineer's spec, and does the work get inspected?

Yes. On commercial work we build to the detail and spec your engineer or architect provides — the right material, the specified weld, and the connection drawn on the plan. If the connection needs to be signed off or inspected, we weld it so it's ready for that review. We don't design the structure; we execute the detail you hand us.

Are you licensed and insured for commercial welding?

Yes, Idaho Welding Service is licensed and insured, which matters on commercial sites where a certificate of insurance is usually required before anyone works. If your GC or property manager needs proof of coverage on file before we start, just ask and we'll get it handled.

Can you schedule around the other trades on site?

That's the whole idea. Commercial jobs run on a sequence, and welding usually has to land in a specific window between other trades. Tell us when the work needs to happen and we'll show up in that slot, get our scope done, and get out of the way so the next crew can keep moving.

Do you do after-hours or weekend commercial welding?

Yes. Occupied buildings, storefronts, and warehouses often can't have welding happening during business hours, so we schedule repairs and installs for evenings, early mornings, or weekends when it keeps you open and out of your customers' way. Call 208-409-0756 to line up a time that works around your operation.

How does commercial welding quoting work?

Send the plans, the spec, or photos of what needs to be done along with the site location, and you get a straight quote on that scope — no runaround. Price depends on the material, the amount of weld and fabrication involved, access, and the schedule, so the more detail you send, the tighter the number. You'll know the cost before we roll out.

Need a commercial welder?

Calling is fastest. You can also email or text the plans, the scope, or photos of what needs welded, along with the job site location, and we'll come back with a straight quote and a slot in the schedule.
