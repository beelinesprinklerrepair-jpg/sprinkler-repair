Boise Pressure Irrigation and Canal Districts

Boise has a more complex irrigation landscape than most Treasure Valley cities. Depending on where you live, your irrigation water may come from the City of Boise's pressure irrigation system, one of several canal companies, or an HOA-managed system. The irrigation season in Boise typically runs from April 15 to October 15.

City of Boise Pressure Irrigation System

Subdivisions built in the last 10 years may be connected to the City of Boise's pressure irrigation system. The City operates this system for a specific list of subdivisions. If your neighborhood is not on that list, you are served by one of Boise's irrigation districts instead.

Common Sprinkler Problems We See in Boise

While many irrigation issues are universal, certain sprinkler problems appear more frequently in Boise due to the age of some irrigation systems, mature landscaping, and varying water pressures across different neighborhoods. Some of the most common sprinkler repairs we perform in Boise include leaking sprinkler valves, broken lateral lines and wet spots in yards, low pressure zones, sprinkler heads that continue dripping after a cycle, damaged wiring, and systems that will not shut off completely.

Many of these issues begin as small problems but can quickly lead to dead grass, wasted water, or expensive repairs if ignored. If you are experiencing one of these symptoms, visit our Sprinkler Troubleshooting Center for additional information and repair guidance.
