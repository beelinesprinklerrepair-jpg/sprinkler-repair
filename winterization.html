Sprinkler Blowout & Winterization in Nampa, Boise, Meridian, Caldwell and the Treasure Valley

Water left sitting in an irrigation line through an Idaho freeze expands and it has nowhere to go. PVC pipe, rotor bodies, valve diaphragms — none of it is built to take that kind of pressure. Come spring you are looking at cracked heads, split lateral lines and blown diaphragms scattered across the system. A proper sprinkler blowout gets all of that water out before the first hard freeze hits and saves you a repair bill in March.

We run a commercial grade compressor with the CFM needed to actually clear a multi-zone system all the way through. Every zone gets multiple passes. We hit Nampa, Caldwell, Boise, Meridian, Kuna, Eagle, Star and Middleton every fall and the schedule fills up fast so the earlier you call the better.

What We Do on Every Sprinkler Winterization

  • Hook up commercial grade compressor to the blowout port on your irrigation system
  • Run each zone with multiple compressed air passes until no water is coming out of the heads
  • Keep rotor zones at the right pressure so we do not blow out the Rain Bird 5000 Series rotor seals
  • Confirm all lateral lines and manifold areas are fully cleared
  • Shut off the main irrigation supply valve
  • Set Rain Bird ESP controller to off-season mode
  • Check the backflow prevention device and make sure it is drained before we leave

Why DIY Blowouts Fall Short

Most homeowners who try to blow out their own system use a shop compressor or a small portable unit. Those do not move enough air to clear a full multi-zone system. You might get the heads near the manifold but water sits in the lateral lines 40 or 50 feet out all winter. That is where you find the cracked pipe in April. We bring the right equipment and we do not leave until the job is done. That is the difference between a real sprinkler winterization and a partial one that costs you twice as much in the spring.
