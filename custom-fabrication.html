Custom metal fabrication in the Treasure Valley

When off-the-shelf doesn't fit.

Plenty of jobs start with the same problem: the part you need isn't sold anywhere, or the one that is doesn't quite fit. A mount that's an inch off. A rack that has to clear something specific. A bracket for a machine nobody makes parts for anymore. That's where custom fabrication comes in — instead of forcing a stock piece to work, we build the exact thing for the exact spot.

Idaho Welding Service handles one-off metal work across the Treasure Valley for homeowners, contractors, farmers, and shops. Brackets, mounts, frames, racks, bumpers, skids, steps, guards, and equipment modifications — welding and fabrication built around how the piece gets used, not around what happened to be on a shelf.

Start with a sketch, a photo, or the broken part

You don't need drawings. A phone photo, a napkin sketch, or a few measurements is usually enough to get started. Call or text what you've got and you'll get a straight answer on whether it's buildable, what material makes sense, and roughly what it runs — before anything gets cut.

What we build

One-off metal work, made to do a job.

Custom fabrication covers a wide range, but it almost always comes down to the same thing — a piece of metal cut, fit, welded, and finished to do something specific. If you can describe how it needs to work, it's usually something we can build.

Tell us what it holds and how it gets used, and we'll build a piece that fits the space and takes the load.

Common questions

Custom fabrication FAQ.

What kinds of custom metal fabrication do you build?

One-off builds and modifications — brackets, mounts, frames, racks, bumpers, skids, steps, guards, and equipment mods. If it needs to be cut, fit, welded, and finished in metal, it's the kind of thing we build. Tell us how you'll use it and we build to that.

How much does a custom fabrication project cost?

It depends on the build — the material, the size, how much fitting and welding is involved, and how it needs to be finished. The fastest way to get a real number is to text photos, a sketch, or measurements to 208-409-0756. You get a straight answer before any work starts.

Can you build from a sketch or a photo?

Yes. A napkin sketch, a phone photo of what broke, or a few measurements is usually enough to start. Full drawings are welcome but not required — a lot of custom work starts with a rough idea and gets figured out together before anything is cut.

What metals do you work with?

Mild steel for most structural and equipment work, stainless where it needs to resist rust or stay clean, and aluminum where weight matters. We'll talk through which material fits the job and how you'll use it so the build holds up in the real world.

Can you fabricate a replacement part that's no longer made?

Often, yes. When a part is obsolete or the replacement is backordered, we can measure the old one — or what's left of it — and fabricate a piece that fits and works. Repair-by-fabrication keeps older equipment running when parts just aren't available anymore.

Do you build on-site or in a shop?

Both. Modifications and repairs to equipment that can't move are handled on-site with mobile welding. Standalone builds — racks, frames, brackets, and the like — are usually built where the tools are and delivered ready to use.

Got something to build?

Calling is fastest. You can also text a sketch, a photo of the part, or measurements, along with where you are in the Treasure Valley, and we'll get back to you with the next step.
