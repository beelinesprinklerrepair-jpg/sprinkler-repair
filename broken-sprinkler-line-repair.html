How We Find a Broken Line

The tricky part of a broken underground line is knowing where exactly it broke. We start with a pressure test to isolate the affected zone and confirm there's a leak. From there we can usually narrow the break to a section of pipe, which lets us dig one hole instead of ten. Once we've exposed the break we cut it out, replace the damaged section with quality pipe and fittings, and pressure test again before we backfill.

Most breaks happen at fittings, elbows, or tees — places where the pipe was stressed over time or hit by frost heave. Older systems with poly pipe that's dried out and cracked are also common in Caldwell, Kuna, and older neighborhoods across Boise and Nampa.

Signs You Have a Broken Sprinkler Line

  • Soft, soggy, or muddy area of the yard that doesn't dry out between watering cycles
  • One zone running at noticeably lower pressure than the others
  • Water pooling near the backflow preventer or meter when the system isn't running
  • Dead or dry strips next to a wet section — loss of coverage downstream from the break
  • Water bill noticeably higher than normal
  • Zone that runs but doesn't appear to water the yard effectively

Main Line vs. Lateral Line Breaks

There are two types of underground line breaks we deal with. A lateral line break is within a specific zone — the pipe that runs from the valve out to the sprinkler heads. These are generally easier to isolate because only one zone is affected. A main line break is in the pipe that feeds water to all your valves, which usually shows up as water leaking even when no zones are active.

Both are fixable. Main line breaks sometimes involve more excavation depending on depth and location, but the repair itself is straightforward once the break is exposed. We've repaired both types all across Meridian, Eagle, Star, and Middleton.

Common Questions

Broken Sprinkler Line FAQ

How do I know if I have a broken sprinkler line? +
The most common signs are a soft or muddy area of the yard that doesn't dry out, low pressure on one zone, or a section of lawn that stays wet even when the system isn't running. If the main line breaks you may see water pooling near the backflow or meter. A pressure test will confirm the location.
How much does broken sprinkler line repair cost? +
Most broken line repairs in the Treasure Valley run between $150 and $400 depending on the depth, length of the break, and how much pipe needs to be replaced. Main line breaks can run a bit higher depending on depth. Beeline gives you a quote before any work starts.
Can you find the break without digging up the whole yard? +
Yes. We use a pressure test to isolate the zone and narrow down the location of the break. This lets us dig a targeted hole rather than excavating large sections of the yard.
How long does a sprinkler line repair take? +
Most broken lateral line repairs are completed in a few hours. More extensive main line breaks may take longer depending on depth and pipe length, but same-day completion is common.
Will you repair the yard after digging? +
We backfill and tamp the excavated area after the repair. Seeding or sod replacement for the disturbed area is up to the homeowner, but we leave the site clean.
