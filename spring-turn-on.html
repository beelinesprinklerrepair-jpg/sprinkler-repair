Sprinkler Spring Turn-On & Activation — Treasure Valley, Idaho

Spring in the Treasure Valley arrives fast. Temperatures climb, grass starts growing, and most homeowners haven't thought about their irrigation system since October. Turning your system back on without a proper inspection first is one of the most common ways to miss freeze damage that runs all season — wasting water, running up your bill, and leaving dry patches in your lawn while you wonder what's wrong.

A proper spring turn-on isn't just flipping a switch. We do a zone-by-zone activation, walk the entire property checking every head for freeze damage or misalignment, verify valve operation, check the backflow device, and program your controller with the correct spring schedule. You start the season knowing your system works — not hoping it does.

What We Do on Every Spring Turn-On

  • Slowly pressurize the system to check for main line leaks before activating zones
  • Activate each zone and walk the property checking every sprinkler head
  • Replace any heads cracked or damaged by freeze cycles over winter
  • Adjust rotor arc and spray radius for proper lawn coverage
  • Check all irrigation valves — zones that are slow to activate or won't shut off
  • Inspect backflow prevention device for winter damage
  • Program your controller with the correct spring watering schedule
  • Walk you through the full system before we leave

Why a Professional Spring Turn-On Is Worth It

Idaho winters are hard on irrigation systems. Freeze-thaw cycles crack heads, shift lateral lines, and stress valve diaphragms even on systems that were properly blown out in the fall. A professional turn-on catches these issues before they run unnoticed all spring. Finding a cracked head in March costs a fraction of what it costs in July after it's been spraying sideways for three months.
