Why a Professional Spring Startup Matters

Turning on your sprinklers in spring is not just opening a valve. If the main shutoff is opened too fast, the sudden pressure surge — called water hammer — can crack PVC fittings, split lateral lines, and blow apart unions. We open the system slowly to let pressure build gradually and avoid that kind of shock damage.

After the long Idaho winter, freeze damage is also common. A head that cracked over the winter might not show obvious signs until you run that zone and water starts pooling in the wrong place. We run every zone visually so we can spot broken heads, low-pressure zones, misaligned rotors, and obvious leaks before you end up with a dead spot or a soggy mess mid-summer.

When to Turn On Sprinklers in the Treasure Valley

The general window for spring startup in the Treasure Valley is mid-March through mid-April, depending on overnight temperatures and when your water source comes online. Here is how it breaks down by water type:

  • City of Nampa pressure irrigation — typically turns on in early to mid April. Watch for the City's announcement each spring.
  • City of Boise pressure irrigation — typically begins around April 15.
  • Settlers Irrigation District — serves parts of Meridian and Eagle, generally turns on in early April.
  • NMID (Nampa-Meridian Irrigation District) — canal water delivery typically starts in April depending on snowpack.
  • Potable / domestic water systems — these can be turned on whenever overnight temps are reliably above freezing, often late March to early April.

If you are not sure which system feeds your sprinklers, we can help identify it during the startup visit.

Freeze Damage Found During Spring Startups

We see the same types of damage every spring across the valley — usually on systems where the blowout was skipped or done with an underpowered compressor. Common findings at startup:

  • Cracked or shattered sprinkler heads (water stays in the head body and freezes)
  • Split valve diaphragms (leaves zones stuck on or not opening)
  • Cracked backflow preventer bodies (most expensive to miss)
  • Broken PVC fittings at the manifold or at zone risers
  • Lifted or pushed-up rotor heads from freeze/thaw soil movement

Most of these are fixable on the spot. A cracked head swap takes minutes. A damaged backflow body takes longer but we carry the common parts. If you skipped the blowout last fall, a thorough spring startup is your chance to find out what winter did before it becomes a mid-summer emergency.

Irrigation District Spring Startup Timing

One thing that catches homeowners off guard every spring is the difference between when their water source turns on and when the sprinkler system is ready to go. If your system uses pressure irrigation or canal water, the water simply is not there until the district opens it. Turning on the system with no water supply means the pump runs dry or zones just do not cycle.

Beeline tracks the typical startup dates for the main irrigation districts in the Treasure Valley. When you book a spring startup, we can help you schedule around your district's expected opening date so we are not out there before your water is available.
