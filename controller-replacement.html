Irrigation Controller Replacement in the Treasure Valley

The irrigation controller is the brain of your sprinkler system. It tells every zone when to turn on, how long to run, and how often to water. When it fails, nothing runs correctly — zones stop activating, schedules reset on their own, or the system runs at the wrong times.

Beeline has been diagnosing and replacing irrigation controllers for homeowners across Nampa, Boise, Meridian, Caldwell and all of the Treasure Valley since 2015. Before recommending a replacement, we assess your existing wiring, zone count and system type to make sure the new controller is compatible with what you already have installed. Most controller replacements are completed in a single visit.

How Controllers Fail Over Time

Sprinkler controllers are exposed to heat, cold, moisture and power surges year after year. Most residential controllers are rated for 10 to 15 years, but many fail sooner — and a lot of homeowners in the Treasure Valley are running systems with controllers that are 15 to 20 years old.

One of the most common failure signs is the LCD screen. The display will start going dim, showing partial characters, or go completely blank even when the unit still has power. At that point you can not read the schedule, confirm zones are set correctly, or tell whether the system is even running. Some controllers will keep running a ghost schedule for a while — watering at the wrong times or skipping zones — without giving any obvious sign something is wrong.

Internal components also degrade. Capacitors wear out, circuit boards corrode from humidity, and zone output terminals stop sending a signal. A controller might work fine for one zone and fail completely on others. Once the electronics start going, repair is usually not practical — a replacement is cleaner and more reliable.

Signs Your Sprinkler Controller Needs to Be Replaced

Blank or Dim LCD Screen

The display goes dark, shows partial characters, or fades out even with power connected. A dead screen means you can not confirm or change anything.

Zones Not Turning On

One or more zones stop activating on schedule even though the valves and wiring check out fine.

Programs Keep Resetting

The controller loses its schedule after power outages or simply will not hold programming between sessions.

System Runs at Wrong Times

Zones fire in the middle of the night or at random times despite the schedule being set correctly.

Zone Outputs Failed

Certain zone terminals no longer send a signal even when the wiring and solenoids test fine.

Controller Is Very Old

Controllers over 10 to 15 years old are usually more cost-effective to replace than repair, especially when parts are no longer available.

Controller Repair vs Replacement

Not every controller problem requires a full replacement. Sometimes the issue is a wiring fault, a failed solenoid on a specific zone, or a programming error that can be corrected on site. We diagnose the actual problem first. If the controller itself is the cause and repair is not practical, we will tell you and explain what a replacement involves before any work begins.

If the problem turns out to be wiring or a valve rather than the controller, see our sprinkler repair service page for more on those types of fixes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my sprinkler controller needs to be replaced?
If zones are not turning on, the LCD screen is blank or partially blank, programs keep resetting, or the controller will not hold a schedule after a power outage, it likely needs replacement. LCD screens degrade over time and can go fully dark even when the unit still has power — at that point the controller is not reliable regardless of what is happening beneath the surface.
What sprinkler controllers does Beeline install?
We install controllers from major residential irrigation brands common throughout the Treasure Valley, including standard timer-based controllers and WiFi-enabled smart controllers like the Rain Bird WiFi series that connects directly to your phone.
Will a new controller work with my existing sprinkler system?
In most cases yes. We assess your existing wiring and zone count before recommending a replacement so the new controller is compatible with what you already have in the ground.
Can I control a WiFi controller from my phone?
Yes. WiFi-enabled controllers like the Rain Bird series connect to your home network and can be managed from an app on your phone. You can adjust schedules, run zones manually, and get weather-based skip alerts from anywhere.
