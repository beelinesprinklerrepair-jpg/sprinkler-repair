Common Sprinkler Problems We Fix

Broken Sprinkler Heads

Rotor heads and pop-up spray heads get hit by mowers, cracked by freeze cycles, and clogged by debris. We replace heads matched to your system's pressure and zone layout.

Irrigation Valve Failures

A zone that will not turn on or will not shut off is almost always a valve problem. We diagnose whether it is a worn diaphragm, a failed solenoid, or a wiring issue and fix the actual cause.

Wiring and Electrical Faults

Zones that do not respond are not always a bad valve. Wire breaks, corroded connectors and failed solenoids are common and frequently misdiagnosed. We trace the fault to the source.

Sprinkler Controller Problems

Lost programming, dead zone outputs, display failures — irrigation controllers fail in predictable ways. We diagnose and repair or replace controllers from all major brands.

Low Water Pressure

Low pressure across one zone or your whole system points to a main line issue, a partially closed valve, or a pressure regulator problem. We find the cause and fix it.

Irrigation Leaks and Wet Spots

A wet spot in the yard between watering cycles means a valve is bleeding through or a lateral line has a crack. We locate and repair the source before it turns into a bigger problem.

Coverage Problems and Dry Spots

If one part of your lawn stays dry while another area gets soaked, the issue may be a clogged nozzle, bad spray pattern, damaged head, poor adjustment or a pressure problem. We run the zone, find what is being missed and make the adjustments or repairs needed for more even coverage.

Can I Fix a Sprinkler Problem Myself?

Some simple issues, such as cleaning a clogged nozzle or adjusting a spray direction, may be manageable for a homeowner. When the problem involves a leaking valve, underground broken line, wiring fault, controller failure, backflow assembly or water that will not shut off, it is usually better to have the system diagnosed before the problem gets worse.
