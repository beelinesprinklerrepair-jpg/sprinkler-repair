Sprinkler Blowout and Winterization in Nampa

The City of Nampa typically closes its pressurized irrigation system in the first or second week of October each year, when the irrigation districts end their season. Once the city shuts off the water, you cannot run your system again until spring. That makes your blowout window short — plan to get it done in late September or the very first days of October. We use compressed air to clear every zone and confirm your system is fully protected for winter.

See the City of Nampa irrigation season closure notice for the most current shutdown date each fall.

Spring Turn-On in Nampa

Nampa's irrigation system generally comes back online in mid to late April, though the exact date varies year to year depending on when the irrigation districts release water into the canals. After the city brings pressure up, crews flush the lines and test pump stations before water reaches customers at full pressure. That process can take a couple of weeks, so it's smart to have your system inspected and ready to go as soon as pressure is available.

The Nampa Meridian Irrigation District publishes updates each spring on when water will be released into the system. Booking your spring turn-on early means we can get to you right when the water comes on.

Good to Know: City of Nampa Irrigation Reminders

From the City of Nampa Irrigation Division

  • Check your sprinkler controller programming each season to make sure you are not over-watering. Over-watering is a violation of Nampa City Code Section 8-1-22.
  • Do not use your house's potable (drinking) water to irrigate your lawn. The city does not provide bill adjustments if domestic water is used for irrigation, and it will significantly increase your water and sewer charges.
  • When getting your sprinkler lines blown out in the fall, make sure your contractor does not operate the city curb stop valve. That valve is for city emergency use only. Beeline knows this and works around it correctly on every job.
