Mainline Leak Repair

If you're seeing any of the following on your Boise property, there's a good chance you have an underground sprinkler leak that needs attention:

  • A wet spot in the yard that doesn't dry out even on hot days
  • Soggy soil near a valve box or manifold area
  • Water pressure drop on one or more irrigation zones
  • Grass growing noticeably greener and faster in one section of the lawn
  • Foundation or driveway dampness near where irrigation lines run
  • An unexplained spike in your monthly water bill

Any one of these signs warrants a call. The longer an irrigation leak in Boise runs, the more saturated the soil becomes — and in heavy clay soil, that water doesn't go away quickly. It pools, spreads laterally, and causes far more damage than the initial crack.

Mainline Sprinkler Leaks

The mainline is the supply pipe that runs from your water source to the valve manifold. Unlike lateral lines, the mainline is always under pressure when your irrigation is turned on — meaning a break in the mainline causes continuous, significant water loss around the clock.

A mainline sprinkler leak in Boise typically shows up as a consistently wet area near the manifold or along the path the supply pipe follows across the yard. In some cases, water will surface immediately. In others, the water travels underground for several feet before pooling.

Our process for mainline leak repair: we identify the general location by running zones and monitoring pressure, narrow down the break by isolating pipe sections, excavate cleanly at the repair point, replace or splice the damaged section using quality fittings, pressure test the line before backfilling, and restore the disturbed area. We don't charge to start and then expand scope — we quote the job before we dig.

Lateral Line Leaks

Lateral lines run from the zone valves out to individual sprinkler heads. These are the most common location for sprinkler pipe leaks in Boise, and there are several reasons they fail:

  • Freeze-thaw damage from Idaho winters that causes PVC or poly pipe to crack
  • Shifting soil that stresses joints and connections over time
  • Root intrusion from trees and large shrubs working into pipe joints
  • Physical damage from digging, aeration, or landscaping work
  • Age-related brittleness in older polyethylene pipe

Older homes in the North End and Boise Bench often have polyethylene pipe that was installed 20 to 30 years ago. That pipe becomes brittle over time and is prone to cracking at fittings, at tee connections, and along straight runs where soil has shifted. We splice, replace sections, or reroute as needed — whatever the situation calls for to give you a reliable fix rather than a temporary patch.

Why Boise Soil Makes Leaks Worse

University of Washington research on the Boise River watershed found that as the Treasure Valley urbanizes, irrigation drainage patterns are changing. Heavy clay soil common throughout Boise doesn't absorb water quickly — underground leaks tend to pool and spread laterally rather than draining down. That's why a small crack can create a surprisingly large wet area before you see it at the surface.

Sprinkler Leak Repair FAQ — Boise

How do I know if I have an underground sprinkler leak in Boise? +
Signs include a wet spot in the yard that doesn't dry out, unusually green grass in one section, soggy soil near a valve box, higher than normal water bills, or low pressure on one or more zones. If you see any of these, call for an inspection before the problem gets worse. Underground sprinkler leaks in Boise can run for weeks before surface signs become obvious, and the clay soil throughout most of the city holds water longer than you'd expect.
How does Beeline find underground irrigation leaks? +
We start by running each zone and looking for pressure drops, wet areas, and unusual drainage patterns. On stubborn irrigation leaks in Boise, we can narrow down the location by isolating sections of pipe. Most leaks are locatable without tearing up the whole yard. We identify the problem, quote the repair, and get your confirmation before we excavate anything.
How much does sprinkler leak repair cost in Boise? +
Cost depends on where the leak is, how deep the pipe runs, and how much of the line needs to be replaced. Simple lateral repairs typically run less than mainline repairs that require more excavation. We quote the job before we start — no surprise charges after the fact. Call (208) 880-2712 or fill out the form below to get started.
Can a sprinkler leak damage my Boise home's foundation? +
Yes. If a mainline or lateral runs near your foundation, a sustained leak can saturate the soil and cause settling or water intrusion. Boise's clay-heavy soil holds water near the surface rather than letting it drain, which makes this risk more significant than in sandier soils. We take underground leaks seriously for this reason. If you see consistent dampness near your home's base, get it checked quickly.
