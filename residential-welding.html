Common home welding projects

Gates, fences, railings, and repairs.

Gate & fence repair

A gate that sags, drags, or won't latch is almost always a welding problem — a cracked hinge, a broken weld, or a frame that's racked out of square. The same goes for metal fence panels that split at the joints. Most of the time these get welded back to solid right where they hang, no need to pull the whole thing out. It's usually a quick, affordable fix compared to replacing a gate or fence run.

Railing repair & safety

Stair, porch, and deck railings loosen over time — welds fatigue, posts rust at the base, and a rail that used to feel solid starts to give. That's a safety issue worth handling before someone leans on it. Loose railings can usually be re-welded and reinforced in place. On older homes we work to match the existing ironwork so the repair blends in instead of standing out.

Repair or rebuild

How to think about an old piece.

When a repair is enough

Most of the time, metal that's cracked, loose, or broken at a weld can simply be fixed. If the underlying steel is still sound — solid where it counts, just failed at a joint or worn at one spot — re-welding and reinforcing it is faster and costs far less than starting over. A lot of railings, gates, trailers, and yard pieces have plenty of life left once the weak point is addressed. When in doubt, ask before you replace.

When a rebuild makes sense

Sometimes the metal is too far gone — rusted through, bent past straightening, or built in a way that was never safe. In that case a rebuild is the honest answer, and we'll say so rather than patch something that won't hold. On older homes with decorative ironwork, we can also fabricate a new piece that matches the original pattern, so a replacement still looks like it belongs. Send photos and you'll get a straight read either way.

Common questions

Residential welding FAQ.

What is residential welding?

Residential welding is metal repair and custom metalwork for your home and property. That covers everyday fixes like a sagging gate, a broken fence, or a wobbly stair railing, plus small custom builds like brackets, fire pits, and garden structures. It is the same welding work a farm or job site gets, scaled to the projects around a house.

Do you take small welding jobs?

Yes. A single cracked railing, one broken hinge, or a bracket that needs re-welding is worth a call. Plenty of home projects are quick fixes, and those are welcome here. Text a photo of what broke to 208-409-0756 and you will get a straight answer on the fix.

Can you repair a broken gate or fence?

Gate and fence repair is one of the most common calls we get from homeowners. Snapped hinges, cracked welds, bent frames, dragging gates, and rusted-through fence sections can usually be welded back to solid instead of replaced. If a section is too far gone, we will tell you that too.

Should I repair my old railing or replace it?

Often it can be repaired. A loose or rusted railing that is still structurally sound usually just needs re-welding and reinforcing, which costs less than a full rebuild. When the metal is rusted through or the design is unsafe, a rebuild makes more sense. Send photos and we will give you an honest read on which way to go.

Do you come to my house to weld?

Yes. Most residential welding happens right in your driveway or yard. The truck carries its own welders, generator, grinders, and steel, so anything bolted or set in place gets welded where it sits. Smaller pieces can also be dropped off if that is easier for you.

What areas do you cover for residential welding?

The whole Treasure Valley for home welding repair and custom work: Boise, Nampa, Meridian, Caldwell, Eagle, Star, Kuna, and Middleton, plus the neighborhoods and properties in between. Not sure if you are in range? Call or text and we will let you know.
