Nampa & Meridian Irrigation District (NMID) — What Meridian Homeowners Need to Know

Many Meridian homes built after 1993 are served by a Pressurized Urban Irrigation System (PUIS) operated by NMID. This irrigation water comes from the Boise River via canal and is completely separate from your domestic water supply. NMID is responsible for the pump station, main lines, and the first valve on each lot. Everything from that valve to your sprinkler heads is the homeowner's responsibility — that is where Beeline comes in.

NMID Pressurized Irrigation System — Key Facts for Meridian Homeowners

Season & Pressure

The system runs approximately April 15 through early October. Water is delivered at 40–60 psi, filtered to 1/16 of an inch, and is non-potable — for irrigation use only.

NMID Pressurized Urban Irrigation System overview

Low Pressure — What Causes It

Most low pressure issues stem from too many users running at the same time (typically 8pm–8am) or a clogged inlet filter. NMID recommends an odd/even address watering schedule to spread demand. Check your filter first — a 2-inch housing with a 32-mesh screen reduces clogging. If pressure is still low across your whole yard after that, contact NMID directly before assuming it is a private system issue.

Important Low Pressure Information — NMID (PDF) Full NMID Pressurized Irrigation System brochure (PDF)

Before You Dig

Contact NMID at least two working days before digging near any irrigation lines. If you hit a line without prior notice, you are responsible for all repair costs.

Canal Safety

NMID operates over 500 miles of canals and drains near Meridian neighborhoods. Idaho has one of the highest child drowning rates in the country. Treat canals the same as a road — keep children away from the banks at all times.
