Sprinkler Repair in Boise

Boise has grown by over 64,000 people since 2000 and the metro area just crossed 482,000 residents. Beeline has been working across all of it since 2015. Boise only gets about 12 inches of rain a year, not enough to keep a lawn alive from June through September without irrigation. When something goes wrong you want someone who can get there fast and fix it right the first time.

The North End and Older Boise Neighborhoods

The North End has tree-lined streets, older homes, Hyde Park, Harrison Boulevard and the blocks around Camel's Back Park. A lot of those properties have irrigation systems that are 20 to 30 years old and have never really been looked at. Heads that have been hit by mowers, valves that are worn out, PVC that has been through a lot of Idaho winters. The Bench and older parts of Southeast Boise are the same story. We do a lot of work in these neighborhoods and a spring turn-on inspection catches most problems before they turn into a flooded yard in August.

New Construction and Newer Boise Neighborhoods

Southwest Boise and the areas off Cole Road and Overland have seen a lot of new construction. Builder-installed irrigation is often done fast to hit a deadline and we regularly find coverage gaps, overloaded zones and backflow devices that aren't installed right. If you moved into a new Boise home in the last five years and haven't had someone walk your system zone by zone, it's worth doing.
