Nampa & Meridian Irrigation District (NMID) — What Meridian Homeowners Need to Know

Many Meridian homes built after 1993 are served by a Pressurized Urban Irrigation System (PUIS) operated by the Nampa & Meridian Irrigation District. NMID currently provides pressurized irrigation water to approximately 15,000 parcels of land across the Treasure Valley. If your property is on this system, your irrigation water comes from the Boise River via canal — not from your domestic drinking water supply. The two systems are entirely separate.

NMID is responsible for the pump station, the main lines throughout your subdivision, and the first valve off the main on each individual lot. Everything downstream of that valve — the private lines, valves, and sprinkler heads in your yard — is the homeowner's responsibility. That is where Beeline comes in.

NMID Pressurized Irrigation System — Key Facts for Meridian Homeowners

Season & Pressure

The NMID system typically runs from approximately April 15 through the first week of October, depending on water supply. Water is delivered at 40–60 psi — equivalent to a municipal system — and filtered down to 1/16 of an inch. Design your sprinkler heads and filters to accommodate that particle size. The system is non-potable canal water and is for irrigation use only.

NMID Pressurized Urban Irrigation System overview

Low Pressure — What Actually Causes It

Most low pressure problems on NMID systems are caused by overuse during peak watering windows (typically 8pm to 8am) or a clogged inlet filter on your private system. The pressurized system is designed to deliver a set flow rate per property — when too many users run sprinklers at the same time, pressure drops across the whole subdivision. NMID recommends an odd/even address watering schedule to spread demand across the full 24-hour day. If your filter is the culprit, NMID recommends a 2-inch filter housing with a 32-mesh screen to reduce how often it needs cleaning. If you have ruled out both of those and pressure is still low across your entire yard, call NMID's shop at (208) 466-0663 before assuming it is a private system problem.

Before You Dig

Call NMID at least two working days before digging anywhere near irrigation main lines. If you do not call and you hit a line, you are responsible for all repair costs. This applies to landscaping projects, fence posts, and any other ground disturbance near buried irrigation lines.

Canal Safety

NMID operates more than 500 miles of canals and drains throughout southwestern Idaho. Those canals run through or near many Meridian neighborhoods. Idaho has one of the highest accidental drowning rates for children under 5 in the country, and NMID has been working on public safety outreach around canal hazards since the early 1990s. If your property backs up to or is near an NMID canal or drain, treat it the same way you would a road — keep children away from the banks and educate them about the current strength and steep sides even in canals that look calm.
