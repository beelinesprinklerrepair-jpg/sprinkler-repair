Sprinkler Blowout and Winterization in Nampa

The City of Nampa typically closes its pressurized irrigation system in the first or second week of October each year. Once the city shuts off the water, you cannot run your system again until spring. That makes your blowout window short — plan to get it done in late September or the very first days of October. We use compressed air to clear every zone and confirm your system is fully protected for winter.

See the City of Nampa irrigation season closure notice for the most current shutdown date each fall.

Good to Know: City of Nampa Irrigation Reminders

From the City of Nampa Irrigation Division

  • Check your sprinkler controller programming each season to make sure you are not over-watering. Over-watering is a violation of Nampa City Code Section 8-1-22.
  • Do not use your home's potable (drinking) water to irrigate your lawn. The city does not provide bill adjustments if domestic water is used for irrigation.
  • When getting your sprinkler lines blown out in the fall, make sure your contractor does not operate the city curb stop valve. That valve is for city emergency use only. Beeline knows this and works around it correctly on every job.
