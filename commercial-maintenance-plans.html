Commercial Irrigation Maintenance Plans — Treasure Valley

Most commercial irrigation problems aren't emergencies that come out of nowhere. They build up over a season — a head starts misting instead of throwing, a valve loses its tight shutoff, a controller program drifts from what was set. Regular maintenance catches those issues before they become a flooded common area or a dead turf section in front of a retail center. Beeline offers commercial maintenance plans that cover your property across the full irrigation season, with the same crew and the same contact every time.

🌸

Spring Activation

  • Full system pressurization and zone-by-zone run
  • Head inspection and adjustment across all zones
  • Controller programming review and seasonal update
  • Backflow test and documentation
  • Valve function check — open, close, response time
  • Written zone report left on file
☀️

Mid-Season Inspection

  • Zone-by-zone run to check coverage and pressure
  • Head adjustment for seasonal growth changes
  • Controller runtime check against current conditions
  • Identification of any developing issues
  • Minor repairs handled on the spot
  • Notes on anything needing follow-up
🍂

Fall Winterization

  • Compressed air blowout — all zones cleared
  • Controller shut-down or winter programming set
  • Valve inspection before winter shutdown
  • Main line drain verification
  • Backflow insulation check where applicable
  • System ready for Idaho winter

Every Commercial Property Is Different

We don't sell commercial maintenance off a fixed menu because no two properties have the same needs. An 8-zone HOA common area is a different job than a 35-zone apartment complex with multiple buildings and a shared main. Zone count, property size, access requirements, and how the property has been maintained in prior years all factor into what a plan looks like and what it costs.

When you call about a commercial maintenance plan, we'll talk through what you have — number of zones, property type, what's been done in the past — and put together a plan that actually fits your property. Pricing is negotiated based on the volume and complexity of the work, not pulled from a rate card.

Why a Maintenance Plan Makes Sense for Commercial Properties

💧
Water Cost Control A misdirected head or a slow valve leak adds up fast on a commercial meter. Maintenance keeps the system running at its designed efficiency.
🏡
Landscape Protection Dead turf or flooded common areas affect property values and tenant satisfaction. Regular coverage checks catch problems before they show up on the surface.
❄️
Freeze Damage Prevention A properly blown-out system doesn't crack heads, split laterals, or destroy valves over winter. The cost of a blowout is a fraction of a full freeze repair.
📞
One Contractor Who Knows Your System When a problem comes up mid-season, you're not explaining the property layout to someone new. We already know it.

What Happens When We Find a Problem

If we find an issue during a scheduled visit — a broken head, a failing valve, a wiring fault — we'll document it and talk through the repair. Minor repairs can often be handled the same visit. Larger repairs get quoted and scheduled. You're not locked into a separate service call process; you're working with a contractor who already knows your system and can move quickly when something needs attention.
