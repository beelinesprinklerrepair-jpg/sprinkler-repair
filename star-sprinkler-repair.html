Common Sprinkler Problems We See in Star

Star's mix of newer builder-grade systems, windier conditions, and rapid growth creates a specific set of irrigation problems that Beeline sees regularly. The most common issues we repair in Star include:

  • Builder system coverage gaps and dry corners — Star subdivision systems are frequently under-designed. Wide head spacing and shallow zone budgets leave edges and corners without adequate coverage, especially on larger lots.
  • Overloaded zones from cost-cutting during construction — builders often pack too many heads on a single zone to save on valve costs. The result is low dynamic pressure and poor coverage across the entire zone.
  • Controller programming issues on new homeowners — Star has a high percentage of first-time homeowners unfamiliar with their irrigation controllers. Incorrect run times and zone schedules cause both over- and under-watering.
  • Backflow freeze damage after missed blowouts — Star winters are cold and backflow devices installed in exposed or poorly protected locations are vulnerable when blowouts are skipped or done too late in the season.
  • Spring startup problems after poor winterization — cracked lateral lines, split heads, and failed valves discovered at first spring turn-on are common when the fall blowout was incomplete.

If you are dealing with one of these issues, see our guides on bad spray coverage, low pressure zones, or visit the full Sprinkler Troubleshooting Center for step-by-step diagnosis help.
